3. Group Tests p.260

Majority success check
  • Each participating hero rolls an individual test with an appropriate skill.
  • Result: if half or more succeed, the group succeeds; otherwise it fails.
  • Failures may still cause setbacks at Director’s discretion.
Exact rulebook quote + page
p.260 “Group Test: Two or more creatures attempting to overcome a single, simple task together can make a group test. If half or more of the creatures succeed on their individual test, the group test succeeds. Otherwise the group test fails.”

6. Combat Particularities p.269–279

Falling, forced movement, terrain, position, support
  • Falling (p.270): Fall ≥2 squares → 2 dmg/sq (max 50) & land prone; reduce height by Agility; into liquid (≥1 sq deep) −4 effective squares.
  • Fall onto a creature (p.270): Both take same falling damage; faller lands prone in nearest unoccupied space; if faller’s size > target’s Might → target is knocked prone.
  • Vertical forced move (p.272): If left midair & can’t fly → fall. Against flying targets, forced move is vertical.
  • Slam: creature→creature (p.272): Movement ends; both take 1 × remaining squares damage.
  • Slam: creature→object (p.272): Into unbroken object (size ≥ target): 2 + 1 × remaining; downward also apply falling (Agility 0).
  • High Ground (p.270): Fully above and on ground → gain an edge on the power roll (climb caveat).
  • Difficult terrain (p.270): +1 square to enter; can’t shift into/out of; can’t jump out.
  • Damaging terrain (p.270): Deals listed damage; can’t shift into/out of; can’t jump out.
  • Cover (p.276): Damage‑dealing abilities vs target take a bane.
  • Concealment (p.276–277): Strikes vs target take a bane.
  • Flanking (p.276): Edge on melee strikes when adjacent on opposite sides.
  • Aid Attack (p.273): Choose adjacent enemy → next ally’s ability roll vs it (before your next turn) gains an edge.
  • Catch Breath (p.273, 278): Spend a Recovery → regain Stamina = recovery value; a dying creature can’t use this in combat.
Exact rulebook quotes + pages
p.270 Falling: “When a creature falls 2 or more squares and lands on the ground, they take 2 damage for each square they fall (to a maximum of 50 damage) and land prone… Falling into liquid that is 1 square or more deep reduces the effective height of a fall by 4 squares…”
p.270 Falling onto another creature: “A creature who falls and lands on another creature causes that creature to take the same damage from the fall… If the falling creature’s size is greater than the Might score of the creature they land on, that creature is knocked prone.”
p.272 Vertical forced movement: “If a creature who can’t fly is left in midair at the end of a vertical forced move, they fall… Forced movement made against a creature who is flying is always a vertical forced move…”
p.272 Slam into creatures: “When you force move a creature into another creature, the movement ends and both creatures take 1 damage for each square remaining in the first creature’s forced movement…”
p.272 Slam into objects: “When a creature force moves a target into a stationary object that is the target’s size or larger and the object doesn’t break, the movement ends and the target takes 2 damage plus 1 damage for each square remaining… If you force move a creature downward… they also take falling damage as if… Agility score was 0.”
p.270 Difficult Terrain: “An area of obstacles that are difficult to move through. It costs 1 additional square of movement to enter a square of difficult terrain. A creature can’t shift into or out of difficult terrain, and can’t jump out of difficult terrain.”

p.273 Catch Breath: “A maneuver that allows a hero to spend a Recovery and regain Stamina equal to their recovery value.”

7. Helping Other Creatures p.258, 273

Assist & Make/Assist Test
  • Assist a Test: Use a relevant skill the actor isn’t using; make a test. Your outcome sets their modifier: T1 bane • T2 edge • T3 double edge.
  • Make or Assist a Test (maneuver): Use a maneuver to make a test or assist another creature’s test during combat.
Exact rulebook quotes + pages
p.258 “You can attempt to assist another creature with a test they make, provided you have a skill that applies to the test, the other creature isn’t using that same skill on the test, and you can describe how your character helps to the Director’s satisfaction… When you attempt to assist another creature, make a test using the skill you choose, and using a characteristic chosen by the Director… The outcome of that test determines the bonus applied to the test you’re assisting: • You get in the way or make things worse. The creature takes a bane on their test. • Your help grants the other creature an edge on their test. • Your help gives the other creature a double edge on their test.”
p.273 “A creature can use a maneuver to make a test or to assist another creature’s test.”

9. Combat Flow & Structure p.265–268, 275

Round/turn economy & core triggers
  • Round (p.265): each creature takes a turn.
  • Surprised (p.266): no triggered/free triggered actions; ability rolls vs them gain an edge.
  • Your turn (p.267): 1 main + 1 maneuver + 1 move; any order; main can convert to maneuver or move; split movement.
  • Triggered actions (p.267): 1/round; free triggered don’t count toward that limit.
  • Free maneuvers (p.267): simple on‑turn tasks; many allowed.
  • Shift (p.268): move 1 square without provoking; can’t shift into/out of difficult or damaging terrain.
  • Opportunity attack (p.275): leaving adjacency without shifting/teleporting → melee free strike.

p.267 “You can use one triggered action per round… A free triggered action… doesn’t count against your limit…”
p.267 “Once all creatures on both sides of a battle have acted, the combat round ends… Director‑controlled creatures act in groups…”
p.275 “When an adjacent enemy willingly moves away… the creature can make a melee free strike as an opportunity attack…”
